## **FAQ**

This is a new promotion and we understand that you might have some questions, so let’s answer them:

**Can I get Vacation Loot any time of year?** *No, this offer is only good for stays between February 1 and March 31, 2026.*

**Can I stay anywhere?** *This offer is only for our participating hotels. You can see the full list*[*<u>here</u>*](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/loot/)*.*

**Why do I have to call the hotel? Can’t I book online?** *To get this promotion, you need to call the front desk of a participating hotel and ask for the Local Vacation Loot promotion. It won’t be applied automatically unless you mention it. Some hotels may have restrictions or blackout dates. Promotion is not valid with other hotel promotions. For more details, visit* [https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/loot/](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/loot/)*.*

**Can I use the $50 vouchers anywhere on Oregon’s Adventure Coast?** *No, only the businesses listed on our*[*<u>Local Vacation Loot</u>*](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/loot/) *page accept them. Be sure to contact them in advance to set up your adventure.*

**Isn’t the Oregon Coast always cold and rainy in February and March?** *Not always! The weather changes a lot in winter, but we get plenty of sunshine too. We can’t predict the weather, but don’t count us out!*

**I already planned a trip and just heard about this. Can I still get Vacation Loot if I show up?** *Sorry, no. The deal isn’t retroactive. You have to mention the promotion when you book. But there’s still time to plan a quick two-night return visit!*

**Why do I need to reserve my adventure in advance?** *Activities like charter fishing, ATV rides, and kayaking fill up quickly. Call the locations directly and book at least a few days ahead. Be sure to mention you’ll be using Local Vacation Loot when you book.*

**What does Local Vacation Loot include?** *Here’s what you get:*

* *Two $50 coupons (one per person) to use toward select charter fishing, ATV rentals, kayak rentals, or dining*
* *Two passes to a participating museum (choose one of three local museums)*
* *20% off one item at the Coos Bay Visitor Information Center or the North Bend Visitor Information Center*

**Wait, Oregon’s Adventure Coast has museums?** *It does, and this package includes admission to one museum of your choice from the following options.*

* [*Coos Art Museum*](https://coosartmuseum.org/)*, the third oldest art museum in Oregon, features rotating exhibitions, touring shows, and a strong collection of Pacific Northwest art. Don’t miss the Prefontaine Memorial Gallery honoring this hometown hero. Visit the Coos Art Museum website for more information.*

  &nbsp;

* *​The* [*Coos History Museum*](https://cooshistory.org/) *is Oregon’s oldest continuously operated historical society, featuring more than 250,000 historic photographs and changing exhibits that highlight the region’s tribes, industries, and coastal history. Visit cooshistory.org for more information.*

  &nbsp;

* [*Charleston Marine Life Center*](https://cmlc.uoregon.edu/)*\: The Charleston Marine Life Center is a hidden gem on Oregon’s South Coast, featuring interactive exhibits like the Marine Ecosystem Gallery, Underwater Oregon Gallery, and an open air Oregon Fisheries Gallery overlooking the harbor, along with touch tanks, whale skeletons, and more.*

**Tell me about the adventures and dining I get to choose from!** *We have partnered with several local outfitters to bring you exciting Oregon Coast adventures. Be sure to book at least several days in advance by calling the locations directly to set up your excursion. Mention that you will be using Local Vacation Loot when you book.*

Looking for a unique adventure? Rent an ATV and ride through the Oregon Dunes National Recreation Area. This spot offers both relaxation and excitement. Stretching 40 miles along the coast, it’s North America’s largest area of coastal dunes, with over 32,000 acres of dunes, forests, streams, and lakes. **What else do you recommend?** *There’s too much to list in this post, but here are some fun ideas for your February and March trip! Be sure to visit our* [Adventures Page](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/adventures/) *and see our* [Trip Ideas](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/tripideas/) *for more inspiration!*

**STROLL ALONG ONE OF THE LOCAL BEACHES.** You might be surprised, but February and March often bring warm, sunny days to the Oregon Coast, perfect for a walk on our beautiful beaches. Watch surfers at Bastendorff Beach or look for treasures in the tide pools at Sunset Bay State Park. WARNING: Be aware of coastal safety hazards like undertows, sneaker waves, and rolling logs. Never turn your back on the ocean. [Learn more.](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/undeveloped-beaches/)

**HUNT FOR TREASURES IN OUR UNIQUE SHOPPING DISTRICTS.** Take some time to browse the unique shops in Downtown Coos Bay, North Bend, and Charleston. [Learn more.](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/shopping/)

**LOOK FOR LADY LUCK IN ONE OF OUR CASINOS.** Did you know our part of the Pacific Northwest has two of the region’s best casinos? Whether you like slots, cards, or other games, you’ll find fun and excitement here. Both casinos are just a ten-minute drive apart, so try your luck at both.[Learn more](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/gaming/).

**CATCH A PERFORMANCE.** Any night of the week, you’ll find talented musicians playing at local venues. Our music scene is relaxed and down-to-earth, true to the Oregon Coast vibe. You won’t find fancy clubs, but you will find great places to support up-and-coming bands. [Click here](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/entertainment-and-nightlife/)for recommendations.

**GO HIKING.** Take a break from the noise and hike through peaceful coastal forests and rugged landscapes along Oregon’s Adventure Coast. Whether you want to climb ancient dunes or walk along paths with Pacific views, there’s a beautiful adventure waiting for you. [Learn more.](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/hiking-walking/)

**EXPLORE THE CAPE ARAGO BEACH LOOP.** Take a scenic drive and spend the day exploring beaches and state parks along the Cape Arago Highway. Watch seals and sea lions at Simpson Reef and Shell Island, part of the Oregon Islands National Wildlife Refuge. Download our guide to help plan your route and choose your own adventure. For full details, get our [PDF Guide for the Cape Arago Beach Loop](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/img/cape-arago-loop-itinerary.pdf). A $10 parking fee applies to our State Parks, but a single fee covers all three parks for the day.
