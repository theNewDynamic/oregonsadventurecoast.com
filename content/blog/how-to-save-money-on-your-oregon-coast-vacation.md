### **Get Outside Without Spending a Dime**

![Man stands paddling a stand-up paddleboard in the calm waters below a steep rocky bluff at Sunset Bay State Park in Coos Bay while two children in life vests sit in front of him.](/img/oregon-s-adventure-coast-on-a-budget.jpg)

This region's natural landscape is the real draw, and much of it is free to explore. Walk the [<u>Coos Bay Boardwalk</u>](https://wheeltheworld.com/accessible-things-to-do/usa/coos-bay/coos-bay-boardwalk?srsltid=AfmBOoppSFM8tgkoARBKAr9TSHABVQqp8qlVWAhMTJ5n22XtyKJxRvBD) and check out the new interpretive signs along the waterfront, complete with audio recordings from community members sharing the area's story.[<u>Hike the trails</u>](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/hiking-walking/) at Empire Lakes, Millicoma Marsh, Mingus Park, the North Spit of Coos Bay, or Simpson Park and Ferry Road Park, all favorites among locals looking to stretch their legs.

For a scenic drive, explore the [<u>Cape Arago Beach Loop</u>](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/tripideas/explore-the-cape-arago-beach-loop/), an eight-mile route connecting [Sunset Bay, Shore Acres, and Cape Arago State Parks.](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/state-parks-and-national-lands/) The [Sunset Bay to Cape Arago Trail](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/oregon/sunset-bay-to-cape-arago) offers a 6.5-mile round-trip hike along the cliffs, with shorter options available. State Parks charge a $10 daily parking fee per vehicle, which covers all parks for the day. The fee provides access to attractions such as Shore Acres gardens and Simpson Beach cove. Visit the Shell Island Interpretive Stop for views of seals and sea lions on Simpson Reef.

If you would rather explore the dunes without renting expensive gear, head to the[<u>John Dellenback Trail</u>](https://www.fs.usda.gov/r06/siuslaw/recreation/trails/john-dellenback-dunes-trail-1339) in Lakeside. As the only non-motorized section of the [<u>Oregon Dunes National Recreation Area</u>](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/untamed-dunes/), it charges just a $5 day-use fee per vehicle. Waterfall lovers should make the trip out to [<u>Golden and Silver Falls State Natural Area</u>](https://stateparks.oregon.gov/index.cfm?do=park.profile&amp;parkId=67), and cyclists can test their skills on the [<u>Whiskey Run Mountain Bike Trails</u>](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/cycling/) through misty forest and fern-covered ground.

Beach days are always free here, whether you choose [<u>Horsfall Beach, Bastendorff Beach, Sunset Bay, Seven Devils, or Whiskey Run Beach</u>](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/undeveloped-beaches/). Just remember that sneaker waves and drifting logs are real hazards, so always keep an eye on the water and never turn your back on the ocean.

### **Taste the Local Flavor**

![A smiling woman wearing a white tank top stands behind a table lined with wine bottles at a &quot;Back Alley Pub &amp; Grill&quot; during the North Bend Sip-N-Stroll.](/img/north-bend-oregon-sip-n-stroll.jpg)

Food does not have to be expensive to be memorable. The [<u>Coos Bay Farmers Market</u>](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/img/Coos-Bay-Farmers-Market.pdf) runs every Wednesday from 9 am to 2 pm through the end of October, and it is free to browse local produce, honey, baked goods, and handmade crafts. Stop by [<u>Cranberry Sweets in Coos Bay</u>](https://cranberrysweets.com/?srsltid=AfmBOoqQBE8AghNUr3oHLN7PpVbrmcfo7wkettzw660xX17u-P5zQ3Mh) to watch candy being made most weekdays while sampling as much as you like. For fresh fish and crab, visit [Capt. John's Fisherman's Wharf](https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=61574447611196) on D Dock in Charleston, and if you are visiting in July or August, keep an eye out for wild blackberries growing along the roadside, free for the picking.

Visitors 21+ can enjoy sampling wine as they explore local downtown businesses for just a $10 donation to a local nonprofit at the [North Bend Sip N Stroll](https://www.facebook.com/events/2137038826855427) in July. If you are unable to attend that event, the [<u>Coos Bay Downtown Wine Walk</u>](https://www.facebook.com/CoosBayWineWalk) offers a similar experience on the first Friday of each month for only $10.

### **Catch a Free Show or Festival**

&nbsp;

Summer here means live music almost every week. The [<u>Oregon Coast Music Festival</u>](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/event/annual-oregon-coast-music-festival/) brings free concerts and workshops to the area, and [<u>Music on the Bay</u>](https://www.facebook.com/musiconthebay) offers a free concert series at Mingus Park Amphitheater every Thursday evening starting August 13. Bring a picnic and settle in. Festival season also brings the [<u>July Jubilee</u>](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/event/north-bend-july-jubilee/) in North Bend and the [<u>Blackberry Arts Festival</u>](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/event/annual-blackberry-arts-festival/) in downtown Coos Bay in August. In September, the [<u>Salmon Celebration</u>](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/event/salmon-celebration/) honoring Native American heritage and the [Bay Area Fun Festival](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/event/annual-bay-area-fun-festival/) are also very popular with visitors. All are open to the public and free to attend, though there will be opportunities to purchase food and beverages and participate in activities.

### **Dig Into Local History and Culture**

Some of the best ways to learn about this region cost little or nothing at all. The [<u>Oregon Coast Historic Railway Museum in Coos Bay</u>](https://www.facebook.com/OregonCoastHistoricalRailway/) invites you to climb aboard vintage locomotives and explore railroad history up close, with interactive play areas for kids and a gift shop full of train-themed souvenirs. It is open Wednesdays and Saturdays from 11 am to 4 pm, and while admission is free, donations are always appreciated.

Just down the road, the [<u>Marshfield Sun Printing Museum</u>](https://www.facebook.com/p/Marshfield-Sun-Printing-Museum-100071574391773/) showcases vintage printing presses and typesetting tools that tell the story of early newspaper production on the coast. It is open Wednesday through Saturday from 1 to 4 pm, Memorial Day through Labor Day, and is also free; donations are welcome.

For a self-guided option, pick up a brochure at the [<u>Coos Bay Visitor Center</u>](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/contact/) and take a walking tour through Old Marshfield, or explore the [<u>Marshfield Pioneer Cemetery</u>](https://www.coosbayor.gov/community/city-parks/marshfield-pioneer-cemetery) using an access code available there. Both are free ways to connect with the logging and maritime history that formed this area, as well as the deep roots of the [<u>Coquille Indian Tribe</u>](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/tribal-heritage-coquilles/) and the [<u>Confederated Tribes of the Coos, Lower Umpqua, and Siuslaw Indians</u>](https://www.oregonsadventurecoast.com/tribal-heritage-confederated/).

### **Plan Smart and Enjoy More**

A few simple habits go a long way toward stretching your travel budget. Fill your tank before you arrive, drive the speed limit to improve fuel efficiency, and pack snacks or a lunch for the road. If you belong to AAA, AARP, or a similar organization, bring your card, since some local businesses and museums offer member discounts.

Oregon's Adventure Coast proves that a great vacation does not have to come with a big price tag. Between the discounts offered by museums, open trails, local festivals, and easy beach days, there is enough here to fill a week without emptying your wallet. Pack the car, grab a few snacks, and come see what Coos Bay, North Bend, and Charleston have waiting for you this summer.
